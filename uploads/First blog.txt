1.Market value isn’t the same as list price. The list price is a strategy; market value is what buyers are actually willing to pay.
2.Pre-approval comes before shopping. A buyer who’s pre-approved is stronger, faster, and more attractive to sellers.
3.Conditions matter. Most of the time, it’s the buyer who includes conditions, since they act as a safeguard during the purchase process. 
4.Pricing your house higher isn’t always the best. During the first few days your house is on the market is a great window where buyers are excited and agents are looking. You want your house to be priced just right.  
5.Staging and presentation drive price. Clean, bright, well-presented homes sell faster and for more.
6.A REALTOR® protects your interests. You realtor is on your side. They want what’s best for you. 
7.The deposit isn’t the same as the down payment. The deposit shows commitment; the down payment comes at closing.
8.Completion dates and possession dates are different. Legal transfer, payment, and move-in happen on separate but coordinated days.
9.Not all renovations add value. Some updates look great but don’t return the cost when selling.
10. Overpriced homes sit longer, accumulate high days on market, and often need price drops—which signals weakness and invites lowball offers.
11.Professional photos and marketing aren’t optional. Online presentation determines who books showings.
12.Real estate is emotional—but decisions should be logical. Keeping emotions in check during negotiations leads to better outcomes.
